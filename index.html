Failures Along Reasons

1. Lack of Technical Skills

  • Outdated Knowledge: Not staying up-to-date with modern frameworks, tools, or best practices.
  • Shallow Understanding: Only learning surface-level concepts without mastering fundamentals like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, or backend systems.
  • Poor Debugging Skills: Inability to identify and resolve issues efficiently.
  • Ignoring Performance and Optimization: Creating slow or poorly optimized websites that negatively affect user experience.

    • 2. Poor Communication

  • Not Understanding Client Needs: Failure to translate client requirements into functional designs.
  • Lack of Collaboration Skills: Inability to work well with team members, designers, or stakeholders.
  • Overpromising and Underdelivering: Setting unrealistic expectations and failing to meet them.
  • Failure to Adapt:Resistance to adopting new tools or methodologies, such as agile workflows or CI/CD pipelines.

    • 3. Weak Project Management

  • Procrastination: elays in delivering work due to poor time management or lack of prioritization.
  • Inconsistent Workflow: Not following structured processes, leading to messy code and inefficiencies.
  • Scope Creep: Allowing projects to grow beyond the original plan without proper management.
  • Underestimating Marketing: Ignoring the importance of SEO, performance metrics, and user analytics.
